# Personas and Users

This document defines the recurring user types `RiftSense` is meant to
serve.

Feature specs should reference these personas instead of redefining
users ad hoc.

## Primary Personas

## 1. Amateur Team Lead

Who they are:

A coach, captain, analyst, or organizer responsible for helping an
amateur roster improve despite limited shared time.

Primary goals:

- distribute useful learning work asynchronously
- organize team knowledge in a repeatable way
- track whether assigned material was actually completed

Pain points:

- practice time is limited
- educational material is scattered across slides, docs, links, and chat
- follow-through is difficult to monitor without manual effort

Success criteria:

- can package learning into clear modules
- can assign work to a team or subgroup quickly
- can see completion and assessment status without chasing people down

## 2. Time-Constrained Player

Who they are:

A player who wants to improve but cannot spend unlimited time in live
review sessions or unstructured research.

Primary goals:

- consume concise, relevant learning material
- understand what matters most right now
- complete assignments on their own schedule

Pain points:

- too much League content is broad, noisy, or poorly organized
- improvement work often feels disconnected from their actual role or
  team needs
- long live sessions are hard to sustain

Success criteria:

- knows what to study next
- can complete useful learning work in manageable chunks
- sees how content connects to practical team or gameplay outcomes

## 3. Independent Learner

Who they are:

A solo player or self-directed learner using `RiftSense` outside of a
formal team environment.

Primary goals:

- find trustworthy League learning material
- follow a structured path rather than random content consumption
- use quizzes or checkpoints to validate understanding

Pain points:

- public educational content is inconsistent in quality
- it is hard to know what order to learn things in
- passive watching does not always translate into improvement

Success criteria:

- can browse by topic and level
- can move through modules with clear progression
- receives practical learning structure without needing a team lead

## Secondary Persona

## Content Curator

Who they are:

An internal operator or advanced coach who imports, updates, organizes,
and maintains learning content.

Primary goals:

- reuse existing material efficiently
- update content when patch or strategy context changes
- keep the library organized and trustworthy

Success criteria:

- can upload or link assets quickly
- can apply consistent metadata and structure
- can revise content without recreating it from scratch

## Shared User Needs

Across personas, `RiftSense` should optimize for:

- clear organization
- concise and practical learning units
- async completion
- team-aware distribution where relevant
- visible progress and understanding
